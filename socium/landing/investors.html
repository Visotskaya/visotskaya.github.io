SociumTrade for Investors

Investing/trader management

Socium Trade provides investors with an unparalleled opportunity to asses traders and choose the best person/consortium to put funds in or to employ a strategy of a certain trader for investments. Investors can both implement several strategies for different investment portfolios and distribute their resources between a number of traders.

The Socium Trade management suite gives investors comprehensive information on chosen strategies/performance of some trader to estimate efficiency and make rational management decisions.

Our platform reflects performance of every trader so an investor can easily choose a trader to allocate funds in.
We guarantee no intermediaries, no extra fees, and low transaction costs.

Investment terms – smart contracts

All agreements between users on the Socium Trade platform are concluded via smart contracts, which act as intermediaries excluding the human factor and safeguarding investors’ funds.
The capital is stored in smart contracts outside the exchange so no malfunction will harm funds.
Besides, smart contracts clearly determine the work field of a trader keeping him/her within the set terms and preventing him/her from the agreement violations

Investment process

Socium Trade gathers and stores all information on its users, thus enabling investors to find traders by set criteria.
Such as a trader’s rating, expertise, total number of funds in management, percentage of a trader’s own funds in his/her management, risk rate, profitability etc.

Copy trading

Investors can copy an end-of-period asset allocation of a given trader’s portfolio.
With the advanced instrument kit, investors can decide on the most suitable copying strategy and arrange their portfolio (or proportion of it) according to the portfolio of a selected trader(s).

Profiles

Users of Socium Trades have to create an account and specify information about themselves in the profile, which enables them to post and share personal information and professional developments.

Investors indicate their experience, interests, and other relevant information in their profiles, while traders’ profiles reflect their strategies, expertise, performance score etc.

Platform users can see what approaches a trader uses, with whom an investor trusts his/her capital, and how funds are managed. Evaluating the information presented in profiles, investors can pick a trader or trading strategy that suits their investment plans best.

Pools

Socium Trades enables investors to join into pools. These associations allows several participants to give their financial resources into management of another pool participant under the negotiated terms. The head investor, who also allocates his/her capital to the pool, manages all the funds in the pool and usually receives a certain reward for it. The conditions of participation, funds allocation, work rules etc are written in smart contracts.
Pools enables investors to put in their financial resources safely and with minimum efforts and earn passive income.
